What we learned

  • Smart baby monitor market ($1.8B) is below typical VC threshold — they want $10B+ TAMs
  • We struggled to articulate the broader edge-AI platform vision clearly enough
  • Pre-revenue hardware is a very hard VC sell without a working chip-level prototype
  • Consumer validation (Kickstarter traction) matters more than investor pitches at this stage

Our conclusion

We've paused active VC fundraising. Focusing on product + Kickstarter to build the proof that makes future funding easier — and on our terms.

Challenges

What didn't go
to plan

Being honest about the blockers.

⏱ Firmware Port: Slower Than Expected

Porting from Raspberry Pi to LiChee Nano (RISC-V + NPU) is taking longer than planned. Video streaming done. Face detection ~90%. Breathing rate and roll-over still pending — and these are the Kickstarter gate.

💰 VC Fundraising: 0 Term Sheets

27+ investor meetings over 6 months produced no offers. Market size perception and early hardware stage were the main blockers. Significant time investment that we're now redirecting.

👥 Two Founders, One Full-Time Job

As a 2-person team, we're already stretched covering hardware, firmware, AI, product design, business development, and fundraising. The biggest constraint: Max has a full-time job, meaning most development happens in evenings and weekends. We simply can't move as fast as we'd like. More funding = more dedicated time = faster progress.
